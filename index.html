I am an Assistant Professor of Management Information Systems, in The University of Arizona. I obtained my PhD from the Department of Information Systems and Analytics, National University of Singapore.

My research mainly focuses on economics and human behaviors in information technology, particularly in crowd-based innovation platforms. My research broadly builds upon three streams of literature: organization theories/behaviors, IT & entrepreneurship as well as innovation. I extend organization theory from traditional organizations to new forms of business models where innovation-based tasks are primarily involved. My research projects primarily fall into three categories: 1) entrepreneurial learning dynamics and adaptation behaviors (the focus of my dissertation); 2) network externalities and competition in crowdfunding platforms; 3) marketing and design strategies in crowdfunding campaigns. I mainly employ econometrics and machine learning in my research projects.

My PhD dissertation - "The Dynamics of Crowdfunding: An Entrepreneurial Learning Perspective" - focuses on the developmental growth of entrepreneurs by uncovering the across-campaign and within-campaign dynamics in crowdfunding. The first essay investigates the role of prior experience in subsequent crowdfunding performance. Drawing upon the theory of organizational learning, I theorize the entrepreneurial learning effects from fine-grained experience dimensions. The second essay aims to understand how concurrent funding performance affects entrepreneurs' behaviors during the fundraising process. The conceptualization is based on the aspiration-performance feedback theory, originating from the behavioral theory of the firm. Overall, my dissertation suggests that it is paramount for entrepreneurs to obtain timely feedback from the market while meanwhile seek a balance between exploitation and exploration.

I have presented my research at several leading academic conferences/workshops, e.g., International Conference on Information Systems (ICIS) , the Annual Meeting of Academy of Management (AOM), the Workshop on Information Systems and Economics (WISE) , the INFORMS Conference on Information Systems and Technology (CIST) and the Workshop on Information Technology and Systems (WITS), etc. I received Doctoral Dissertation Award of Computing in 2018, Dean’s Graduate Research Excellence Award in 2016 and Research Achievement Award in 2014 from NUS. My dissertation research appears in the Best Paper Proceeding of AOM. I am a Graduate Valedictorian in NUS Commencement Ceremony 2018.

Prior to my PhD studies in NUS, I spent two years as an undergraduate research assistant in Machine Intelligence Labortory at Sichuan University , where I worked on medical image processing platform. I learnt techniques in image processing and developed portals of medical image registration, segmentation and 3D reconstruction.
